About Me

I am a PhD student at the University of California, Merced and a member of the Mi³ Laboratory under Prof. Ross Greer. My research sits at the intersection of autonomous driving, computer vision, and natural language processing — specifically exploring how vision-language models can improve driving scene understanding and safety.

Prior to my PhD, I gained hands-on experience at NASA Glenn Research Center and Army DEVCOM, and built robotics systems spanning SLAM-based navigation and autonomous manipulation.

I am passionate about bridging cutting-edge AI research and real-world deployment in safety-critical autonomous systems.
